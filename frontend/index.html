How it works
01 —
Subject detection
A center-weighted semantic mask identifies the primary subject of the image — face, object, or central element.
02 —
DCT embedding
32 signature bits are encoded into mid-frequency DCT coefficients of the subject region — invisible to the human eye.
03 —
Redundant encoding
The signature is repeated across all subject blocks. Even if 80% of the image is cropped, remaining blocks still hold the mark.
04 —
Majority-vote decode
The decoder reads all blocks, applies majority voting, and computes a confidence score. Above 70% = verified.
